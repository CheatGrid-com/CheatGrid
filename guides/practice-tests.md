# Practice Tests

Once you've read a topic, a practice test is how you check that it actually stuck. Each one turns a cheat sheet into a set of multiple-choice questions, and because the questions are built around the same concepts, taking the test is also a fast, Q&A way to learn.

## What the questions are like

- **One right answer, two tempting wrong ones.** The wrong options aren't random. They're the mix-ups people actually make, so getting it right means you really know it.
- **Every question comes with a "why it matters" explanation**, often with real-world context and a link to the source, so even a wrong pick teaches you something.
- Questions are split into several **numbered mini-tests**, each drawn from about **three sections** (tables) of the source cheat sheet, with a broader **final test** covering three to five. Every test is **named for the sections it covers**, so you can drill one area at a time or work through the whole pool.

## Three ways to take them

Use the toggle at the top of any topic:

- **Practice:** the default. Answer a question, get **instant feedback** and the explanation, then move on. Best for learning.
- **Exam:** exam conditions with no feedback until the end, and picks auto-advance. Best for testing yourself for real.
- **Browse:** read every question and answer with the explanations visible, searchable, with an "On This Page" outline. Best for a quick review or a skim before an exam.

When you finish an **Exam**, you get a results screen with your **score and percentage**, your **time**, and the **XP** you earned. Tap **See your answers** for a full per-question breakdown, with a side **Questions** panel that marks each one right or wrong and jumps you straight to it.

## QA Flashcards

Every Practice Tests topic has a **QA Flashcards** tab that converts its questions into spaced-repetition cards: front is the question, back shows only the **correct answer** and its explanation (the wrong options are left out). They use the same **Review** and **Practice** modes as regular flashcards (see the **[Flashcards guide](intelligent-flashcards.md)** for how those work), so you can drill the whole question set as cards and keep it fresh over time.

## Progress, XP & streaks

Your study time earns **[XP](xp-and-levels.md)**, finishing a test adds a completion bonus, and completing one counts toward your daily **[streak](streaks.md)**. Your completions and times show up on your **[Stats](stats.md)** page. Your answers live in the browser as you go (a refresh starts the test over). Only your completion is saved.

## Free vs. subscription

**Free topics** are fully playable, with every mode and every question. **Paid topics** are previewable once you're signed in: you can sample the **first test** (up to 10 questions) in Practice and Browse to get a genuine feel for it, while Exam mode stays locked. A **Pro / Pro+** subscription unlocks every test in full. (Certifications and Soft Skills are **Pro+**.)

## Tips

- **Learn in Practice, prove it in Exam.** Use Practice to work through a topic, then switch to Exam to see if it really stuck.
- **Read the explanations even when you're right.** That's where a lot of the value is.
- **Enjoy learning by Q&A?** Any topic with a practice test has a **QA Flashcards** tab, so you can study its questions as spaced-repetition cards.
- **Studying for a certification?** Its practice test is built to mirror the real exam. See the **[Certifications guide](certifications.md)**.
