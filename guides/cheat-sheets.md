# Cheat Sheets

Your map of any topic: a whole subject laid out as clean, scannable tables. Every sheet is:

- **Ordered by level:** tables run from basics to advanced.
- **Sorted by usefulness:** inside each table, the most-used rows come first.
- **Backed by examples:** every concept shows a short example of real usage.
- **Beyond the basics:** fundamentals plus advanced patterns and edge cases.
- **Easy to explore:** spot related concepts nearby.
- **Quick to scan:** find the row you need in seconds.

## The 3-column format

Every sheet uses the same structure, so once you've read one you can read them all:

- **Concept:** the thing itself (a technique, command, method, principle, framework, etc.), linked to its best primary source
- **Example:** a snippet, a command, or a formula, so you see how it's actually used
- **Description:** a clear explanation of what it is or does

## Finding your way around

- **🧠 Mind Map:** every sheet has an interactive Mind Map of all its concepts. Skim it to see the whole landscape at a glance, **hover any node** to preview its description and example, then **click it to jump straight to that row** in the tables. You can zoom and pan around it.
- **⚡ Quick Index:** a clickable outline of every sub-table and concept, for fast jumps without scrolling.
- **🔍 Search:** use the search box up top to jump to any topic across the whole library (see the **[Search & Assistant guide](assistant-and-search.md)**). When you open a sheet from a search result, your term is highlighted right in the tables, and you can step between matches.
- **📚 References:** every sheet ends with a categorized reading list, and every concept links to its source, so it's easy to go deeper when you want to.
- **🗓️ Last updated:** each sheet shows the date it was last refreshed, so you can see the content is kept current. The home page also has a **New & Recently Updated** section, so you can spot what's fresh across all topics at a glance.

## Read it your way

Tune the layout from the controls in the top bar:

- **Text size:** make the tables bigger or smaller with the **A / A** buttons.
- **Tables side by side:** show more tables across the screen at once, or fewer, with the **Tables** buttons. Handy on a wide monitor when you want to compare more at a glance.

## Copy what you need

Select and copy any row or cell straight into your own notes or docs. It keeps its **formatting and colors**, so it pastes cleanly into apps like Microsoft Word or Google Docs, ready to build your own reference as you go. Want to keep something for later? **Save it to [My Vault](my-vault.md)** with a click of the **★ star** button.

## One topic, more than one tool

Many topics come as a set. When a topic also has flashcards or a practice test, you'll see a small **"For this topic:"** row of quick links near the top, so you can explore the sheet, then **study it with [flashcards](intelligent-flashcards.md)** or **test yourself with a [practice test](practice-tests.md)** without hunting through menus.

A selection of cheat sheets is **free**. Look for the "Free Access" picks on the home page. A **Pro** or **Pro+** subscription unlocks the full library and everything we add in the future. (Soft Skills and Certifications are part of **Pro+**.)

## Tips

- **Start with the big picture.** Skim the mind map to get your bearings, keep the sheet open while you work, then come back to check the details as you need them.
- **You don't have to read every row.** The top of each table is the part you'll reach for most, so start there and go deeper when you want to.
- **Going deep on a topic?** Save the key rows to My Vault and turn the topic into a study plan with its flashcards and practice test.
