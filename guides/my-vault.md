# My Vault

My Vault is your personal corner of CheatGrid: the place to keep the rows you care about and build your own cheat sheets out of them.

## Save the rows you reach for

Reading a cheat sheet and want to keep a row? Hit the **star** and it's saved to your Vault. Your saved rows are grouped by their source topic, and you can **search, filter, and reorder** them (both the rows within a topic and the topics themselves), so your Vault stays organized the way *you* think. You can even give it a **color theme** of your own.

Each saved row is a snapshot, so it stays put even if the original sheet changes later. And when a source *does* change, **"Check for updates"** tells you and lets you pull the new version in if you want it.

## Build your own cheat sheets

Beyond saving rows, you can create **custom tables**, your own from-scratch reference sheets, with the same concept / example / description format. They even come with their own **auto-generated mind map**, just like the built-in sheets. Organize everything into named **subpages** to keep different projects or subjects apart, and **move any saved row** into another subpage or table whenever you want to rearrange things.

**Edit Mode** lets you rewrite the cells of a saved row in your own words, handy for adding your own notes or simplifying something for future-you.

## What's included with each plan

- **Free:** save rows and organize your Vault.
- **Pro:** much higher save limits, custom tables, and more subpages.
- **Pro+:** the highest limits, plus Edit Mode for rewriting saved cells.

(Exact limits are shown in the app and on the **[pricing page](https://www.cheatgrid.com/subscription)**.)

> A heads-up: My Vault is for organizing references, not for storing sensitive or confidential information.

## Tips

- **Make a "study" subpage** per topic you're learning and collect the key rows there.
- **Build a custom table** for things that live across several sheets, your own personal cheat sheet.
- **To actually remember what you save,** study the topic's **[flashcards](intelligent-flashcards.md)** and **[practice tests](practice-tests.md)** alongside your Vault.
