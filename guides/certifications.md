# Certifications

Studying for a professional certification? CheatGrid has dedicated, exam-focused prep for many of the most in-demand ones: cloud, security, data & AI, project management, finance, and more.

A certification is a special kind of topic: instead of a single sheet, it bundles everything you need to prepare, all built around the exam's actual objectives. It's the same building blocks as the rest of CheatGrid (cheat sheets, practice tests, flashcards), but pointed at one goal.

The difference is in what shapes them: a normal practice test is built from the concepts in its cheat sheet, while a certification's materials are guided by the **official domains and coverage** of that specific exam, so what you study lines up with what you'll actually be tested on.

And it's built to help the material actually *stick*, not just be read once. The flashcards lean on concrete, worked examples that make tricky ideas click, and the explanations in practice tests bring in real-world context, so you're not just memorizing terms but understanding how they show up in practice.

## What each certification gives you

- **📋 A cheat sheet:** the whole exam blueprint laid out as structured tables with Quick Index and Mind Map, so you can see every domain and concept at a glance.
- **📝 A practice test:** questions written to mirror the real exam's style and difficulty, with explanations for why each answer is right and why this matters in real world. They're split into named mini-tests, each covering a few of the exam's sections, with a broader final test.
- **🎴 Flashcards:** for many certifications, a concept-based spaced-repetition deck with simple, beginner-friendly explanations and concrete examples, so the material sticks between now and exam day.
- **🃏 QA flashcards:** the same study flow, but built from the practice-test questions and answers, so you can drill the exam-style Q&A as cards too.

Across all of these, concepts, questions, and cards **link out to their source**, so whenever you want to dig deeper or double-check something, you're one click away.

Find them all on the **[Certifications page](https://www.cheatgrid.com/certifications)**, grouped by category, with a filter box to jump to a specific one fast.

## Ways to study each piece

Both the practice test and the flashcards give you a few modes via a toggle at the top, so you can switch between *learning* and *testing yourself*:

**Practice test** (more in the **[Practice Tests guide](practice-tests.md)**)
- **Practice:** answer, get instant feedback and the explanation, move on. Best for learning.
- **Exam:** exam conditions with no feedback until the end, then a full per-question breakdown. Best for proving you're ready.
- **Browse:** read every question, answer, and explanation, searchable. Best for a quick skim.

**Flashcards** (more in the **[Flashcards guide](intelligent-flashcards.md)**)
- **Review:** your daily "what to study today" list, made of due cards plus a few new ones, on a smart schedule that brings each card back right before you'd forget it.
- **Practice:** drill a batch right now, ignoring due dates. Great just before the exam.
- **Browse:** read and search every card, no grading.

## A study plan that works

1. **Skim the cheat sheet and its mind map** to map out the exam and spot your weak areas, and **star** the tricky rows as you go, so they collect in your **[My Vault](my-vault.md)** as a personal review list for the concepts you want to revisit before exam day.
2. **Run the flashcards daily** so the material moves into long-term memory instead of being crammed the night before.
3. **Drill the practice test in Practice mode**, reading every explanation.
4. **A few days out, take it in Exam mode** under real conditions to find any last gaps.
